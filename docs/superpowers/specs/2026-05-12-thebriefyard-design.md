## 1. North Star

**Single sentence.** thebriefyard is the practice engine for designers: the deepest, most discoverable, most shareable combinatorial brief generator on the market — no AI, no paywall, no friction.

**What we are.** A free web app that combines human-curated slot grammar into millions of reproducible practice briefs, with permanent URLs, branded export, and total SEO coverage of every (job × industry) pair plus curated examples.

**What we are not (permanent non-goals):**

- Not an LLM generator. Permanent philosophical decision (LD-002), in the spirit of the original Goodbrief.io.
- Not a B2B briefing platform for real client work (that is Brieff/Coda/Notion territory).
- Not a marketplace of paid briefs.
- Not a creative project-management tool.
- Not a social network for designers in v1 (community layer is roadmap, v2/v3).
- Not a mobile or desktop app, in any version (LD-011). PWA installable is the ceiling.

**12-month success criteria (objective is A1: live product with traffic):**

| Metric                                                    | 12-month target                  |
| --------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Google-indexed pages                                      | ≥ 6,000 (covers full v1 surface) |
| Organic clicks per month (Search Console)                 | ≥ 30,000                         |
| Monthly sessions                                          | ≥ 50,000                         |
| Briefs generated per day                                  | ≥ 1,500                          |
| Permalink share clicks (unique outbound from /brief URLs) | ≥ 500 / week                     |
| Average SERP position for "design brief generator"        | ≤ 3                              |
| Monthly uptime                                            | ≥ 99.9%                          |
| Monthly infrastructure spend                              | ≤ US$ 50                         |

**Comparisons we accept.** Goodbrief.io, FakeClients, Brandbrief, Sharpen.
**Comparisons we reject.** Briefly, FakeBrief, Nikhara — AI-first generators, different category.

## 2. Vocabulary (canonical)

Use these terms exactly. No synonyms. New terms require ADR.

- **Slot** — a category of interchangeable content fragments (e.g. `emotions`, `audiences`, `colors`).
- **Slot entry** — a single fragment within a slot (`{text, weight, tags?, contributor?}`).
- **Template** — a sentence with `{{slot}}` placeholders, lives in a job or industry file.
- **Job** — type of design work (logo, packaging, website, etc.). 15 in v1.
- **Industry** — vertical context (tech, food, fintech, etc.). 20 in v1.
- **Hub** — the landing page for a (job, industry) pair: `/brief/[job]/[industry]`. 300 in v1 × 2 locales.
- **Brief** — the generated artifact: company name, description, deliverable description, deadline, optional expanded fields. Has a deterministic `seed`.
- **Seed** — 6-character base36 string. The PRNG initial state. `(job, industry, locale, contentVersion, seed)` → exactly one Brief, byte-stable across runs.
- **Permalink** — a URL of the form `/brief/[job]/[industry]/[seed]` that resolves to a specific Brief.
- **Curated seed** — a seed hand-picked by the editor, listed in `curated-seeds.json`, indexable, present in the sitemap.
- **Ad-hoc seed** — a seed generated by a user click, **`noindex, follow`**, shareable but not in sitemap.
- **contentVersion** — monotonic integer; bumps when slot semantics change.
- **Guide** — long-form editorial article: `/guides/[job]` or `/guides/industry/[industry]`. Authored prose, not generated. v1 fase 2.
- **Locale** — `en` or `pt`. Locales are autoral (not machine-translated).
- **Expanded mode** — opt-in toggle producing additional brief fields (competitive context, creative constraint, mood words, forbidden words).

## 5. Landscape Decisions (LD)

LDs are strategic. Change requires founder + architect review, not just a code PR.

- **LD-001 — Open core.** App MIT. Slot corpus CC BY-SA 4.0. No AGPL.
- **LD-002 — Zero LLM permanent through v3.** Philosophical, explicit. No "AI generate", no "AI expand", no "AI explain" in v1 or v2.
- **LD-003 — SSG-first until > 500k pageviews/month.** Threshold defined in ROADMAP §9.
- **LD-004 — Bilingual from v1.** EN + PT-BR launched together. Each locale is autoral, not machine-translated. ES is v2 if PT validates.
- **LD-005 — No auth in v1.** Permalink is deterministic seed in URL. Local history via IndexedDB only.
- **LD-006 — No paid tier in v1 or v2.** Single sponsor-of-the-month slot is the only monetization considered.
- **LD-007 — Do not compete in "AI-generated briefs".** Different category. Copy explicitly says human-curated.
- **LD-008 — Single-region (Vercel default) until > 100k MAU outside the deployed region.**
- **LD-009 — SEG-E (Community) activation gate.** ≥ 30k organic clicks/month for 2 consecutive months AND ≥ 1,500 briefs/day AND ≥ 50 verifiable "save/share/profile" user requests.
- **LD-010 — SEG-G (Contribution) activation gate.** Slot corpus stable for 6 months AND ≥ 5 external contributors opening PRs of acceptable quality.
- **LD-011 — Web only.** No iOS, no Android, no Electron. PWA installable is the ceiling.
- **LD-012 — Zero invasive tracking.** Plausible (cookieless) + Vercel Web Analytics only. No GA4, GTM, Meta Pixel, Hotjar.

### 6.2 Canonical flow — generate a brief

1. User loads `/brief/logo/food` (SSG HTML, includes 10 curated examples + interactive generator + 200-400-word editorial blurb).
2. Click "generate new" → POST `/api/brief` `{job:"logo", industry:"food", locale:"en"}`.
3. Route Handler invokes `generateBrief({job, industry, locale})` from `packages/core`. No seed = generate new 6-char base36 seed.
4. Response `{seed, brief, url:"/brief/logo/food/a7f3c2"}`.
5. Client `history.replaceState` to the new permalink. Permalink is now valid forever.
6. Visiting the permalink later: SSG (if curated) or ISR (ad-hoc, on-demand cached) renders the same brief deterministically.

### 6.3 Deterministic generation contract (ADR-004)

```typescript
function generateBrief(input: {
  job: JobId; // one of 15 literal strings
  industry: IndustryId; // one of 20 literal strings
  locale: 'en' | 'pt';
  seed?: string; // 6 chars [0-9a-z]; if omitted, cryptographically random
  contentVersion?: number; // default = current
  expanded?: boolean;
}): Brief;
```

Algorithm:

1. Resolve seed. If omitted, `crypto.randomBytes(4)` → base36 → take 6 chars.
2. Initialise PRNG: `mulberry32(seedToInt(seed))`. Never call `Math.random`.
3. Load compiled content for `(locale, contentVersion)`.
4. Pick industry template via `prng.pickWeighted`.
5. Fill `{{slot}}` placeholders by weighted random from `industry.slots[name]`.
6. Generate company name: `prefix + core + suffix` from three slot arrays (autoral, **not Wordlab**).
7. Pick job template; fill `{{slot}}` placeholders from `job.slots`.
8. Pick deadline.
9. If `expanded`, fill optional slots.
10. Validate result against the `Brief` Zod schema; throw on mismatch (content bug, fails CI).

Guarantees:

- Pure, no IO, no side effects.
- For a fixed tuple `(job, industry, locale, seed, contentVersion, expanded)`, `generateBrief()` returns a byte-identical `Brief` on any machine, today and in 2030, while the corresponding `content.<contentVersion>.json` artifact exists.
- Target performance: ~50 µs per call.

### 6.4 SEO surface area (P3 — traffic engine)

| Page type                     | URLs in v1                    | Indexable?         | Content rules                                                                                                                                                                  |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------- | ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Home                          | 2 (`/`, `/pt`)                | yes                | Generator + value prop + curated examples list                                                                                                                                 |
| Hub `/brief/[job]/[industry]` | 600 (300 pairs × 2 locales)   | yes                | Mandatory 200-400-word editorial blurb (humans write, from `industries.blurb` + `jobs.blurb` composed), 10 curated example links, FAQ schema, internal links to 4 related hubs |
| Curated permalink             | 6,000 (10 per hub × 600 hubs) | yes                | Brief, OG image, share, "generate another", breadcrumb                                                                                                                         |
| Ad-hoc permalink              | unbounded                     | **noindex,follow** | Shareable, OG-rendered, not in sitemap, not in robots filter                                                                                                                   |
| Job guides                    | 30 (15 × 2 locales)           | yes (v1 fase 2)    | 1,500-2,500 authored words                                                                                                                                                     |
| Industry guides               | 40 (20 × 2 locales)           | yes (v1 fase 2)    | 1,500-2,500 authored words                                                                                                                                                     |
| About / FAQ / Newsletter      | 6                             | yes                | FAQPage JSON-LD on FAQ                                                                                                                                                         |
| **Total v1 (after fase 2)**   | **~6,700 URLs**               |                    | vs ~4 on the original Goodbrief.io                                                                                                                                             |

Each indexable URL carries: `<link rel="canonical">`, `hreflang` pair (en↔pt), unique OG image, unique title, unique meta description, route-appropriate JSON-LD.

JSON-LD per page type:

| Page              | Schema.org types                                 |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| `/`               | `WebSite` + `SearchAction`                       |
| Hub               | `CollectionPage` + `BreadcrumbList` + `ItemList` |
| Curated permalink | `CreativeWork` + `BreadcrumbList`                |
| Guide             | `Article` + `BreadcrumbList` + `Author`          |
| FAQ               | `FAQPage`                                        |

Web Vitals targets: LCP ≤ 1.5 s on 4G, CLS ≤ 0.05, INP ≤ 200 ms. Fonts self-hosted via `next/font`. No CDN-cascade.

Internal linking: each hub links to (a) 4 related job-same-industry hubs, (b) 4 related same-job-other-industry hubs, (c) job guide, (d) industry guide, (e) 10 curated permalinks. Permalink links breadcrumb hub + "generate another" + locale alternate. Minimum 5 internal links per indexable page.

### 6.5 Permanence and export (CAP-3)

- OG image per permalink: Edge route `/api/og/[seed]?j=&i=&l=`. Cache 1 year (seed is immutable for fixed `contentVersion`).
- PDF export: `/api/export` POST `{seed, job, industry, locale, format:"pdf"|"png"}`, Node runtime, react-pdf templated, branded.
- PNG export: same route, Edge runtime via satori, 1200×630 share size or 2480×3508 (A4 print).
- Server re-generates from seed; no need to receive payload.
- No storage of generated assets — on-the-fly.

### 6.6 Monetization (LD-006, v1)

Single "Sponsor of the month" slot in global footer + one spot per guide. Hand-selected. No affiliate tracking, no auction. Sole purpose: cover domain + Supabase Pro if traction crosses free-tier limits. Not a dependency.

### 9.3 Postgres tables (v1 only — minimal)

```sql
create table brief_log (
  id uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  seed varchar(8) not null,
  job text not null,
  industry text not null,
  locale char(2) not null,
  content_version int not null,
  generated_at timestamptz not null default now(),
  source text not null check (source in ('ssg','random','api','share')),
  user_agent_hash text                    -- truncated sha256, no IP, no PII
);
create index brief_log_seed_idx on brief_log(seed);
create index brief_log_job_industry_time_idx on brief_log(job, industry, generated_at desc);

create table brief_reaction (
  id bigserial primary key,
  seed varchar(8) not null,
  reaction text not null check (reaction in
    ('love','meh','reroll','export-pdf','export-png','share')),
  occurred_at timestamptz not null default now()
);
create index brief_reaction_seed_idx on brief_reaction(seed, reaction);
create index brief_reaction_time_idx on brief_reaction(occurred_at);
```

v2 adds `app_user`, `saved_brief`, `response`, `vote`, etc. Out of scope here.

### 9.4 Content versioning

`contentVersion` is a monotonic integer in `packages/content/version.ts`. Any PR changing slot semantics (add / remove / rename / weight change) MUST bump it. PR checklist enforces this. Each compiled artifact (`compiled/content.<v>.json`) is preserved so old permalinks remain reproducible.

Old permalinks resolve with the version they were generated under. A `?v=N` query param overrides. Without `?v`, the latest version is used (ISR rebuilds).

### 9.5 Content quality tests (CI gates)

Located in `packages/content/__tests__/`:

- `schema-valid` — every JSON parses against Zod.
- `parity` — every slot present in `en/` is also in `pt/` (structure, not text).
- `no-duplicates` — no entry text appears in two different slots within the same locale.
- `forbidden-terms` — a maintained list of slurs / harmful stereotypes is checked; any match fails CI.
- `length-bounds` — each entry text within `[1, 280]` chars.
- `smoke-1000` — generates 1,000 random `(job, industry, locale, seed)` combinations and validates the resulting `Brief` against schema. 100% pass required.

## 10. Phase plan

v1 ships in two phases, ~10 weeks each.

### 10.1 Phase 1 — "Generator + 600 hubs" (weeks 1–10)

**Goal.** Live product. EN only. 300 (job × industry) pairs operational, 6,000 indexable URLs, full SEO scaffolding, deterministic generator, interactive UI, export. No PT, no guides, no community.

**Deliverables.**

1. Monorepo Turborepo + pnpm: `apps/web`, `packages/core`, `packages/content`, `packages/types`, `packages/eslint-config`.
2. `generateBrief()` in `packages/core` with TDD: ≥ 95% unit coverage, smoke 1,000 passing.
3. Slot corpus EN: 15 jobs × 20 industries × ≥ 30 entries per slot. Authored by the founder. Reviewed before merging.
4. Routes shipped:
   - SSG: `/`, `/brief/[job]/[industry]` (300), `/brief/[job]/[industry]/[seed]` (3,000 curated).
   - Runtime: `/random`, `/api/brief`, `/api/og/[seed]`, `/api/export`, `/sitemap.xml`, `/robots.txt`.
   - Static: `/about`, `/faq`, `/newsletter`.
5. UI: shadcn/ui base, dark mode, PWA installable, axe-core clean.
6. SEO scaffolding: canonical, hreflang headers ready for PT (inactive content side), JSON-LD on five page types, sitemap.xml dynamic and partitioned at > 5,000 URLs.
7. Plausible + Vercel Analytics + Sentry wired.
8. CI gates: lint, typecheck, vitest, content-lint, playwright e2e, lighthouse audit, sitemap crawler.
9. Deploy: Vercel + Supabase free, `thebriefyard.com` live, `randombrief.com` 301 → primary.
10. Docs: README, getting-started, LANDSCAPE.md, ROADMAP.md, SPEC.md, CLAUDE.md, STATE.md, ADR-001…010.

**Out of phase 1.** PT-BR, editorial guides, expanded mode, regenerate-slot, brief history, community, sponsor slot. (Newsletter Buttondown embed is in phase 1 — it's a static HTML embed with zero infra cost.)

**Binary acceptance criteria (every box must check).**

- [ ] `pnpm install && pnpm test` passes on a fresh clone, no env setup beyond `.env.example`.
- [ ] `pnpm build` produces ≥ 6,000 static pages.
- [ ] Lighthouse mobile on `/`, one hub, one curated permalink: Performance ≥ 90, SEO 100, Accessibility ≥ 95, Best Practices ≥ 95.
- [ ] `generateBrief({job,industry,seed,locale,contentVersion})` returns byte-identical output across 100 invocations (regression script).
- [ ] 1,000 random briefs all pass `Brief.parse`.
- [ ] Permalink: generate → copy URL → open in private window → identical brief renders server-side.
- [ ] PDF export: valid PDF v1.4+ magic bytes for any valid seed.
- [ ] PNG export: valid PNG signature.
- [ ] OG image: p95 first-paint < 500 ms (Edge cache hit).
- [ ] Sitemap.xml lists 6,000+ URLs and returns 200; no broken links in spot-check of 50 random entries.
- [ ] Curated permalinks indexable; ad-hoc seeds carry `<meta name="robots" content="noindex,follow">`.
- [ ] Coverage: `packages/core` ≥ 95%, `apps/web` route handlers ≥ 70%, `packages/content` validation 100%.
- [ ] `main` branch CI green on the last 5 commits AND green for at least 14 consecutive days before declaring phase 1 done.

### 10.2 Phase 2 — "PT-BR + guides + permanence UX" (weeks 11–20)

**Goal.** Double the indexable surface. Open PT-BR market. Ship editorial. Improve retention UX.

**Deliverables.**

1. Slot corpus PT-BR: structural parity with EN, **autoral text** (not machine-translated). Same content lints pass.
2. Routes `/pt/*` live, hreflang fully active.
3. 30 job guides + 40 industry guides (15 + 20, × 2 locales), each 1,500–2,500 authored words, ≥ 5 internal links, Hemingway readability ≤ grade 9.
4. Expanded mode toggle (V7): `?expanded=1` query renders extra fields.
5. Regenerate-slot: click an individual field to reroll only that slot, client-side, PRNG forked from current seed.
6. Local history via IndexedDB: last 50 briefs visible at `/history`. Client-only, no account.
7. Sponsor-of-the-month slot in footer + one per guide.
8. Buttondown newsletter form on home + end of every guide.
9. Activate Supabase `brief_log` and `brief_reaction` ingestion. Internal admin dashboard at `/admin` behind basic auth (env-driven).
10. Formal external a11y audit (paid or peer); fix all critical findings.

**Binary acceptance criteria.**

- [ ] Sitemap grows from 6,000 to ≥ 14,000 URLs.
- [ ] PT slot corpus parity test: 100% of slot keys present, 0 missing translations of UI strings.
- [ ] Each guide ≥ 1,500 words, unique, ≥ 5 internal links, Hemingway grade ≤ 9.
- [ ] Search Console: average CTR > 2% across hub pages over a 30-day window.
- [ ] MAU ≥ 5,000 (Plausible).
- [ ] Briefs/day ≥ 200 (Supabase log).
- [ ] Bounce rate on hubs < 65%.
- [ ] axe-core: 0 critical, 0 serious findings on top 10 pages.

## 12. Testing strategy

| Layer        | Tool                        | What it covers                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| ------------ | --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Unit         | Vitest                      | Generator: determinism (1,000 iterations same seed → same output), PRNG distribution (chi-squared on 10k draws), slot picker weighting, template filling errors. Content: schema validation, parity, forbidden terms.                             |
| Integration  | Vitest + Supabase local     | Loader compiles `locales/*` correctly. `/api/brief` round-trip. `brief_log` ingestion.                                                                                                                                                            |
| E2E          | Playwright (`apps/web/e2e`) | Home → generate → seed in URL → reload → identical render. Hub lists curated examples. Permalink renders server-side (no JS required). PDF export → magic bytes. PNG export → signature. axe-core: no critical violations. i18n PT route renders. |
| SEO crawler  | Custom script (`tests/seo`) | Each indexable page: canonical correct, hreflang pair, OG complete, JSON-LD type matches expectation, internal-link count ≥ 5. Sitemap entries valid, no orphan URLs in build, ad-hoc permalinks carry noindex.                                   |
| Lighthouse   | Lighthouse CI in CI         | Performance ≥ 90, SEO 100, A11y ≥ 95, Best Practices ≥ 95 on 5 reference routes.                                                                                                                                                                  |
| Content lint | Vitest custom matchers      | All Zod schemas pass. EN/PT parity. No duplicates. No forbidden terms. Length bounds.                                                                                                                                                             |

Inviolable rules:

- TDD in `packages/core`. Test first, code second.
- No mocking the generator in route-handler integration tests.
- No CI test against external live sites.
- Snapshot tests only for structure (JSON-LD shape), never for full payloads.

## 14. Anti-patterns and prohibitions

See SPEC §14 in the deployed docs for the full table. Highlights:

| Anti-pattern                                   | Why it kills the product                  | Do instead                                       |
| ---------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| `Math.random()` anywhere in the generator path | Breaks determinism                        | `packages/core`'s seeded mulberry32              |
| Slot added without updating Zod schema         | Build silently breaks runtime             | Schema is source of truth                        |
| Authoring slot text inside TS source files     | Contributors can't review                 | JSON with schema                                 |
| Machine-translating EN to PT                   | Robotic content kills the differentiation | Authored per-locale                              |
| Headless Chromium for OG                       | Cold start, cost                          | satori at the Edge                               |
| Adding Redis "for cache"                       | Violates ADR-002                          | Vercel cache + ISR handle scale until > 500k MAU |
| Logging IP                                     | LGPD / GDPR risk                          | Truncated UA hash only                           |
| `any` in TypeScript                            | Defeats the type system                   | `unknown` + narrow                               |
| Hardcoding the 300 hubs in a manual list       | Unmaintainable                            | `generateAll(jobs, industries)` at build         |
| LLM-generated slot entries                     | Violates LD-002                           | Author by hand                                   |
| Ad-hoc seed in sitemap                         | Bloat + thin content                      | `noindex` on ad-hoc                              |
| OG image cache < 30 days                       | Wasted compute (seed is immutable)        | Cache 1 year                                     |
| Skipping hreflang because PT is "coming later" | Confuses Google, hurts ranking            | Wire hreflang from day 1                         |
| Blocking UI on export call                     | Bad UX                                    | Loading state + cancel button                    |

## 15. Risks and mitigations

| Risk                                               | Probability | Impact | Mitigation                                                                                                                                                          |
| -------------------------------------------------- | ----------- | ------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Authoring the slot corpus takes longer than coding | High        | High   | Phase 1 ships EN only; PT is phase 2. Optionally contract a design copywriter for one sprint.                                                                       |
| AI-first competitors capture the SERP              | Medium      | High   | Explicit "human-curated, no AI slop" copy. LD-007. Attack PT-BR where AI competition is thin.                                                                       |
| Google does not index 6k URLs effectively          | Medium      | High   | Strong SEO foundation (canonical, schema, hreflang, internal links, Web Vitals). 6-month review: if < 5k organic clicks/month, reduce surface and reinvest in P2.   |
| Moral conflict with the original Goodbrief author  | Medium      | Medium | (a) No text copy, (b) different name & visual identity, (c) cordial outreach before public launch, (d) credit Goodbrief as inspiration in `/about`.                 |
| Wordlab heritage carries over inadvertently        | Low         | Medium | Slot corpus is authored from scratch; ADR documents the prohibition on importing Wordlab lists.                                                                     |
| CC BY-SA contamination concern from third parties  | Low         | Medium | Content and code licenses are separate. BY-SA only forces derivative content to share-alike, not code. Re-evaluate to CC BY 4.0 if commercial reuse becomes a goal. |
| SSG build time exceeds Vercel's 45-minute limit    | Low         | Medium | Benchmark at week 3 with 1k pages. If trending high, paginate `generateStaticParams` and let ISR cover the long tail.                                               |
| OG image quota on Edge                             | Low         | Low    | Curated permalinks pre-rendered at build; ad-hoc cached forever.                                                                                                    |
| External a11y audit reveals systemic issues        | Medium      | Medium | axe-core in CI from day 1; pay for a formal audit in phase 2 before launch.                                                                                         |
| No traction post-launch                            | Medium      | High   | Hard binary metrics in §10.3 force honest review. Launch plan: Product Hunt + Hacker News + designernews + Layers podcast outreach + indie communities.             |

## 18. Glossary of common questions for the agent

**"Can I add Redis for caching?"** No. ADR-002. Vercel cache + ISR handles up to ~500k pageviews/month. Open an ADR with measured evidence if you believe the threshold is hit.

**"Can I use an LLM to expand briefs?"** No. LD-002 is permanent through v3. Decline politely and reference this section.

**"Customer asked for paid tier."** Not in v1 or v2 (LD-006). Document the request; revisit at v2 exit.

**"A slot doesn't fit the schema."** Update the Zod schema first (ADR-010). PR fails CI otherwise.

**"Cliente / community member wants to contribute a slot."** v3 (SEG-G). For v1, document the request. For now, contributions are author-only.

**"Should I optimize this query?"** Probably not. Wait for evidence. Trace viewer-equivalent (Vercel Analytics + Supabase logs) tells you what is slow.

**"Phase says X is out of scope but feature seems trivial."** Out of scope means out. Add to backlog. Phase scope is a contract, not a suggestion.
